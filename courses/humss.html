What's HUMSS?

The HUMSS strand focuses on the study of history, philosophy, literature, and social sciences. Students will learn critical thinking, research, and communication skills through various subjects. This strand provides practical experience through internships and community service, and prepares students for careers in education, law, social work, and government.

Learnings

Here are some things you can learn about Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS):

Study history, philosophy, literature, and social sciences
Learn critical thinking, research, and communication skills
Gain practical experience through internships and community service
Prepare for careers in education, law, social work, and government

Career

Job Opportunities

Teacher

Plan and deliver lessons to students, assess their learning, and provide feedback. Create a positive and supportive learning environment, and develop engaging and effective teaching strategies.

Lawyer

Represent clients in legal proceedings, provide legal advice and guidance, and research and interpret laws and regulations. Advocate for justice and fairness, and uphold the rule of law.

Social Worker

Assist individuals, families, and communities in overcoming social and economic challenges, provide support and counseling, and connect them with resources and services. Advocate for social justice and equality.

Government Admin

Oversee the operations and policies of government agencies and departments, manage budgets and personnel, and develop and implement programs and initiatives. Serve the public interest and promote effective and efficient governance.
